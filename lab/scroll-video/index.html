Scroll scrubs the world.

Scroll down and the timeline runs forward. Scroll up and it runs backward. Stop, and it freezes exactly where you left it. One mechanism — no play button, no reverse-decode hack.

Forward on the way down

Scroll position maps to video.currentTime. Down = time increases. The motion is lerped sub-frame for silk, never stepped.

Reverse on the way up — seamlessly

The video is encoded keyint=1 (every frame a keyframe), so seeking backward is instant. That's the difference between buttery and janky.

Paused when still

No scroll, no target change — the rAF loop settles and stops. Frozen frame, zero CPU. too fast to be so excellent.
