01 Executive Summary

A strong foundation, with room to lead the way ministries meet people online.

World Changers Church International already presents one of the cleaner, more contemporary church websites in its peer group. This document is not a critique of that work — it is a stewardship question: given the reach God has entrusted to this house, is the digital front door doing everything it can to remove friction for the seeking visitor, the faithful giver, and the believer who lives on a phone?

We audited worldchangers.org against a ten-dimension rubric distilled from the best digital experiences in the American church — Life.Church, Elevation, Saddleback, NewSpring, Transformation Church, and the platforms they run on (Subsplash, Pushpay, Tithe.ly, Planning Center). The findings are encouraging and actionable: the site is solid and current, and the gaps that remain are the same gaps that separate a very good church site from a category-leading one.

4 Key Gaps
High-leverage gaps: native app, events off-site, giving friction, personalization.

"Blanketing the world with the Gospel of Jesus Christ to meet the needs of mankind." The digital experience is now one of the largest blankets a ministry holds — and it should be as seamless as the in-person welcome at the World Dome.

The headline. The most material gaps are not visual — the site already looks current. They are structural: there is no branded mobile app (the daily front door for the leading churches), the events calendar lives on an external Dropbox link rather than inside the site, giving is functional but lacks the modern one-tap conveniences (Apple/Google Pay, text-to-give), and the experience offers little personalization for a returning member versus a first-time guest. Each is fixable, and each compounds reach.

What we recommend. A phased path — quick wins that bring the calendar and giving conveniences in-house within weeks, followed by a strategic move to a unified app-and-web experience that meets members where they already are: on mobile, every day. The remainder of this document states the current strengths fairly, scores each dimension transparently, and lays out that roadmap.

02 Current State Snapshot

What worldchangers.org does well today.

Credit where it is due. A fair audit of the live site shows a design that is clean, contemporary, and professional — generous white space, modern type, clear hierarchy. It is functional and current. The strengths below are real and should be preserved through any redesign.

Strengths confirmed in the audit

  • Clean, modern design. White space, contemporary type, and a professional, on-brand feel throughout.
  • Clear primary navigation. Our Church · Events · Connect · Media · Resources, with Visit Campus, Join, eChurch, Member Portal and Contact.
  • Prominent giving. A header GIVE CTA to give.worldchangers.org, the Grace Gang Partner program, and a "How to Give" path.
  • Live & on-demand worship. A "Watch Live" streaming section plus latest sermon titled with date and duration, and "Ways to watch."
  • Clear service rhythm. Sundays 10 AM EST · Wednesdays 10 AM & 7 PM EST stated plainly.
  • Responsive & multi-channel. A working mobile nav toggle, plus eChurch and Member Portal pathways.
  • Compelling events programming. Health & Wellness Conference, Father & Son Car Show, Biblical Equality Roundtable — strong, featured content.

Observed friction points

  • Navigation duplicated. Overlapping links across header and body create choice fatigue and dilute the primary path.
  • Limited section differentiation. Sections look similar; the eye is not guided to the next most important action.
  • No mobile app. The persistent, daily connection point used by leading churches is absent.
  • Calendar lives off-site. The 2026 Event Calendar is a downloadable Dropbox link, not a native, browsable calendar.
  • Minimal personalization. Every visitor sees the same page — no distinction between a first-time guest and a returning partner.
  • Outreach under-featured. The mission to "meet the needs of mankind" is under-surfaced relative to its centrality.

WCCI is not starting from behind. It is starting from solid and current — which means the path to best-in-class is shorter than for most churches its size. The opportunity is to convert a good website into a living digital ministry platform.

04 Prioritized Recommendations

A phased path — quick wins first, then the strategic build.

Sequenced so the ministry sees value quickly and low-risk, then moves to the higher-leverage platform work. Nothing here disrupts the in-person rhythm at the World Dome — it extends it.

Phase I

Quick Wins

Weeks 1–4 · Low effort, high trust
  • 1Bring the calendar in-house. Replace the Dropbox download with a native, browsable events calendar — filter by campus/category, RSVP, and one-tap add-to-calendar.
  • 2Add modern giving conveniences. Enable Apple Pay / Google Pay, text-to-give, and surface recurring giving prominently inside a WCCI-branded donation portal.
  • 3Add an "I'm New" funnel. A single high-contrast guest path above service times that captures first-time-guest details and sets expectations.
  • 4De-duplicate navigation. Consolidate overlapping links into one clear primary path and sharpen the visual hierarchy between sections.
Phase II

Core Experience

Months 2–4 · Platform foundation
  • 5Searchable media library. A true on-demand archive — filter by series, topic, speaker, date — with follow-along sermon notes and recommended next messages.
  • 6Interactive livestream. Add live chat, in-stream prayer requests, give-in-stream, and next-step prompts to the Watch Live experience.
  • 7Accessibility pass. Document a WCAG 2.1 AA conformance effort — captions, alt text, keyboard navigation, focus states, color contrast.
Phase III

Strategic Build

Months 4–8 · Daily front door
  • 8Branded native mobile app. One WCCI app: watch live + on-demand, give, sermon notes, prayer, events, and push notifications — the persistent daily connection point.
  • 9Personalized experience. Tailor the page and app to first-time guest, returning member, or partner — surfacing the right next step for each.
  • 10Campus & region picker. Let visitors choose among the five GA campuses and international offices (UK, South Africa, Australia, India) for tailored times and content.
  • 11Feature outreach. Give the "meet the needs of mankind" mission a dedicated, dynamic home for serve sign-ups and community impact.

05 The Opportunity
· Stewarding the reach

Every removed click is a removed barrier between a person and the Gospel.

Since 1986 — from eight people in a school cafeteria to the 8,500-seat World Dome, five Georgia campuses, and offices on four continents — WCCI has stewarded extraordinary reach. The digital experience is now the widest blanket the ministry holds. This work is about making that blanket as seamless as the welcome inside the Dome.

Steward the reach

Meet ~500 staff and a global congregation where they already are — on mobile, every day — through one branded app and a unified web home.

Remove friction

Make giving one tap and the first visit effortless. Fewer barriers to generosity and to walking through the door for the first time.

Honor the brand

Preserve the clean, current design WCCI has already earned — and elevate it to the category-leading tier its mission deserves.

See it, don't just read it — a proposed solution site is ready.

We've built a working demonstration that shows these recommendations applied to WCCI's own brand: native calendar, modern giving, an "I'm New" funnel, and a mobile-first experience. We'd welcome thirty minutes to walk you through it.
