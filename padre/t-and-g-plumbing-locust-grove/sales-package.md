→ Makes sense — that's how you got to 72 reviews. The site catches the people who got your name from a neighbor but then Googled you before calling. Half of them land on a Facebook page and aren't sure you're the real deal. This closes that gap so the referral actually turns into a call.

**I've already got a Facebook page / Google listing.**
→ Right, and that's exactly the issue — that's all you've got, and you don't fully control either one. Facebook buries your phone number and the Google microsite is auto-generated. This is a page that's yours, loads in a second, and is built to do one job: get the customer to call you.

→ That's the whole point — we already built it. You don't manage it, you don't update it. Maybe half an hour of your time total: look over the copy, send me your logo and a couple of job photos, and we publish.

→ Fair. One captured emergency call or water heater install covers most of it — and a single repipe covers it many times over. If the site books even one job this season you'd otherwise have missed, it's already paid for itself.

**I'm not really a tech guy.**
→ You don't have to be. There's nothing for you to log into. It's a phone-first page — the big button just calls your existing number. We handle the technical side completely.
