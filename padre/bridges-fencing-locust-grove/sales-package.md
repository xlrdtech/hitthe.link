## The Setup (why this one closes)

Bridges Fencing is a 30-year operator with a real reputation in Henry County — but their web
presence is broken. The domain they're listed under (bridgesfencing.com) doesn't load on http
**or** https — connection refused, dead/parked domain. Their only live presence is a Facebook
page plus directory scraps (Yelp, Nextdoor, BBB, Manta) and a yahoo.com email.

So the angle isn't "you have no website" — it's softer and truer: **"You've got a page out there,
but it's not pulling its weight. Right now anyone who Googles you and clicks your site gets an
error. We fix that this week."** That's a layup for a 30-year shop that earns by referral and
needs to look as solid online as they are in the yard.

refused both ways. So functionally, a customer who clicks it sees nothing. I'm not selling you a
second site, I'm fixing the one that's broken — and you get to keep it."

"That's exactly why this matters. When someone refers you, the first thing the new customer does
is look you up. Right now they find a dead link, and it makes a 30-year shop look like it folded.
A real site closes the loop on every referral you already earn."

**"$1,500 is a lot."**
"It's one fence job, and it's a one-time cost — not monthly. If this site books you even a single
wood-privacy job this year, it's paid for itself and then some. And you own it outright."

**"I don't have time for this."**
"That's the best part — there's nothing for you to do. It's already built, you've already seen it.
I just need the go-ahead and a few details, and it's live this week. Takes you five minutes."

"Totally fair. The site's live at that link for you to show them — pull it up together. I'll check
back [day]. In the meantime your old link's still dead, so the sooner we flip this on, the sooner
it's working for you."

**"Can you just do it cheaper / monthly?"**
"I keep it simple: one flat build, you own it, no recurring bill. That's actually cheaper than the
monthly site-rental outfits over a year, and nobody can switch your site off on you. $750 to start
