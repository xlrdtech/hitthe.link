FAQ

Common Questions

Does iPad have built-in hands-free control in 2026?
Yes. iPadOS 26 includes native Eye Tracking (front-camera gaze control with dwell-click), Voice Control (full device control by voice, offline), Switch Control (use adaptive switches via Bluetooth), and AssistiveTouch. All are free and built-in under Settings > Accessibility. No extra hardware needed for voice or eye tracking on compatible iPads.
Can I use my Android tablet hands-free?
Yes. Android includes Voice Access (voice control), Switch Access (physical switch navigation), and TalkBack (screen reader). Google added camera-based eye tracking in Android 15+, with further improvements in 2025-2026. Third-party tools like Mouse4all and Sesame Enable offer additional switch and head-movement control layers.
Is eye tracking covered by insurance?
Clinical-grade eye tracking devices like the Tobii Dynavox TD Pilot and TD I-Series are often covered by Medicaid, Medicare (Part B DME), and private insurance when prescribed by a speech-language pathologist or physician for AAC (augmentative communication) needs. Submit via the DME benefit with a Letter of Medical Necessity (LMN). The Tecla Shield switch interface is also frequently covered.
What is the best budget hands-free tablet setup under $50?
The Lamicall Gooseneck Clamp ($20–$35) plus a Bluetooth page-turner remote ($15–$20) gives you a solid hands-free reading/streaming setup for under $55. Pair it with Apple Voice Control (free) on an iPad for full voice navigation without spending extra. For Android, enable Voice Access in Settings.
What mount works best for a hospital bed?
The LEVO G2 Deluxe is the gold standard for bedside use — its 5-axis arm reaches over any bed and holds position reliably. For tighter budgets, the Mount-It! Pole Mount clamps to bed rails. Ensure the clamp jaw opens wide enough (check spec vs. rail diameter before ordering). Also look at dedicated medical overbed tablet arms from companies like Ergotron and Innovative.

Need a fully hands-free accessibility setup?

Start with your iPad's built-in Eye Tracking and Voice Control — both are free and available today in iOS 26 under Settings > Accessibility.
