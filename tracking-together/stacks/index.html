I · The Model

How we price: Stacks, Units & Packages

three terms, one clear structure
🧱
Concept 01

Tech Stacks

The complete set of tools a company needs to operate. There are two kinds: a software tech stack (the apps, sites, and automations) and a hardware tech stack (the physical devices and equipment). The stack is the architecture, what the business actually runs on.

Two kinds → Software stack · Hardware stack
📦
Concept 02

Units

The deliverables, priced. A unit is the tangible thing you pay for and receive, a consultation, a built website system, a configured automation, a report or sheet, a generated video. Units are priced either as concrete outputs or as blocks of time.

A unit is → a thing you receive, priced by output or by time
🎁
Concept 03

Packages

A package is a bundle of the tech-stack components a particular type of customer needs, assembled into one offering. Instead of buying pieces one at a time, a customer gets the right stack of units matched to their situation.

A package = the right stack of units for a given customer type

III · The Principle

Why we price in units

value made explicit

The simple test behind every price

"If you paid for it, what did you receive?"

Unit-based pricing exists to answer exactly that. Every dollar maps to a tangible deliverable, a built website system, a configured automation, a generated video, a block of consulting time. Nothing is vague, nothing is bundled into a fog of "services."

Because each unit is either a concrete output or a defined block of time, the value is visible before you commit and accountable after. You can see what a package is made of, swap units in or out, and scale exactly where it matters. That is the discipline of an orchestration layer: diagnose the gap, prescribe the stack, price it in units you can hold.
