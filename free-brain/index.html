CANON 6 · FREE + UNLIMITED

Free Brain

A tiered $0 LLM path: local MLX first, OpenRouter :free as the fallback brain — no charge, no third-party margin.

The reversal

OpenRouter was D.I.E.'d in xen-race as "paid + 3rd-party margin", and the race harness moved to local MLX ($0). But the :free tier carries neither a charge nor a margin — it voids that exact objection. So free OpenRouter is now canon-6-clean, wired strictly as a fallback, not a hot-path replacement.

Two tiers, first that answers wins

Tier 1 · local MLX — the hot path

Qwen3.5-9B Claude-distilled at :4040. Unlimited, fully private, no network. Primary for every call.

Tier 2 · OpenRouter :free — the fallback brain

Engaged only when the local endpoint is unreachable, or for a heavier brain than the local 9B can reach (up to Nemotron-3-Ultra 550B). $0. Fallback legs are labeled as such so nothing is silently masked.

The client

bin/xen-llm-free.py — stdlib-only, OpenAI-compatible, same idiom as xen-race. Auto tier by default; XEN_LLM_TIER=free|local forces a leg.

xen-llm-free "summarize this"          # auto: local → free fallback
echo "long prompt" | xen-llm-free -
XEN_LLM_TIER=free xen-llm-free "..."   # force the 550B free brain
xen-llm-free --json "..."              # machine-readable for callers

xen-race now falls through to the free brain when a local leg's endpoint refuses — no more hard-fail on a dead :4040. XEN_RACE_NO_FALLBACK=1 forces strict local-only.

Wiring the key (the one human step)

Key resolves from $OPENROUTER_API_KEY, else ~/.config/xen/openrouter.key (chmod 600). Absent → degrades cleanly to local-only.

curl https://openrouter.ai/api/v1/chat/completions \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $OPENROUTER_API_KEY" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"model":"nvidia/nemotron-3-ultra-550b-a55b:free",
       "messages":[{"role":"user","content":"Hello"}]}'

Privacy: on free models, flip the training opt-out in OpenRouter → Privacy settings before sending anything sensitive — free endpoints are more likely backed by providers whose terms permit logging.
