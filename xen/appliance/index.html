# PRD — XEN APPLIANCE: Self-Provisioning HOTL Comms Engine

**Status:** canon 2026-07-17 · engineering estimates labeled as such · voice layer verified real on disk

---

## 1. Product one-liner + who it's for

**A licensed, self-deploying AI receptionist appliance: a small business fills out one web form, and 60 seconds later an AI answers their phone, texts, and unified inbox — with zero terminal, zero QR-scan, zero keychain relink, ever.**

**Who:** B2B service businesses that live on inbound calls/texts and can't afford a receptionist — barbershops, salons, trades, clinics, small agencies ("Diamond Cutz generalized"). Sold as a licensed engine (SEL S-corp IP), not an outcome. Secondary: B2C self-serve at the low rung of the angel ladder.

---

## 2. Chosen architecture + why it beat the others

**WINNER: `appliance-selfhost` two-plane split, grafted with the `hybrid-managed-core` Close-Kit provisioning gate and the `saas-multitenant` auth taxonomy.**

### The scoreboard

| Design | Kills hell (a) | Buildable (b) | Cost (c) | Scale 100s (d) | Anti-fragility (e) | **Total** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| saas-multitenant | 6 | **4** | 7 | **4** | **3** | **4.8** |
| **appliance-selfhost** | 4 | 5 | **8** | **6** | 3 | **5.2 (winner)** |
| hybrid-managed-core | 5 | **3** | 7 | 6 | 4 | **5.0** |

### Why appliance-selfhost wins on the axes that decide the business

- **It is the only design that survives the M4 fragility fact.** saas-multitenant scored a fatal **3/10 on (e)** and **4/10 on (d)** for one reason all three judges circled: it bets hundreds of paying tenants on the single M4 brain that my own memory index documents swapping, wedging, and booting at 0G. "When M4 wedges, EVERY tenant goes dark." The appliance's **fat data plane on the *customer's* cloud** structurally eliminates the correlated-outage SPOF and the noisy-neighbor blast radius. Blast radius = one tenant.
- **It has the best cost-to-serve, honestly earned.** Judge gave it **8/10 on (c)** — the highest cost score of any design — because pushing the data plane onto the customer's bill makes our marginal infra ~$0.40/customer and directly fixes the thesis-level problem: *recovery was unsellable because we ate the ops cost.*
- **It scales structurally, not aspirationally.** **6/10 on (d)** vs saas-multitenant's 4 — stateless shared control plane, per-customer isolated data plane. No "re-architect onto a cluster at 100s" trap.
- **hybrid-managed-core was strategically identical but scored lower on buildability (3/10)** — it re-imports the containerized-multi-tenant-on-our-infra problem that collides with canon ("no local Docker on M4 / one brain / free-first"), and its own judge flagged the same. The appliance dodges that collision by construction.

### What I graft in (the synthesis, not a pure pick)

All three judges independently named the **same two fatal flaws** that a pure appliance-selfhost does NOT fix — so I graft:

1. **From hybrid-managed-core → the Close-Kit provisioning gate.** Provision fires ONLY on `paid + verified` (YES→sign[DocuSign]→pay[Stripe]→verify[Stripe Identity+Plaid]→done). Prevents unpaid boxes and grounds the funnel in existing business canon.
2. **From saas-multitenant → the explicit 3-class auth taxonomy** as the governing rule, plus the **fleet-update story** (the appliance's #3 unaddressed gap: 200 customer-owned boxes on 200 versions). Fix: the licensed image is a **pinned tag pulled from GHCR at boot**; configurator checks-in a version beacon; control plane can pin a new tag + trigger rolling redeploy. Self-host does not mean un-patchable.
3. **The QR-bridge amputation, made explicit** (all three judges' #1 fatal flaw). v1 ships **voice + Telnyx SMS/RCS + OAuth channels only** — every channel that is machine-refreshable. Consumer WhatsApp/iMessage/Signal are **NOT in v1**, not a checkbox that lies. They return later only as a proven, paid add-on. This kills the exact leg that made recovery unsellable rather than deferring-and-disclaiming it.

## 3. Self-provision core (MVP centerpiece)

### 3.1 Form — captures **intent, never secrets** (3 blocks)

Every channel is a **"Connect" OAuth button**, never a login field. Everything technical is *derived*.

- **A · Identity:** business name, timezone, receptionist persona name, business hours, greeting line.
- **B · Channels:** `[Connect Gmail]` `[Connect Google Voice]` `[Connect Instagram]` — OAuth popups only. (v1: OAuth + telephony. No consumer-QR checkbox.)
- **C · Phone & routing:** bring-a-number OR provision a Telnyx DID; IVR tree; human-escalation number; plan tier.

### 3.2 JSON emitted — `xenspec/v1`

```json
{
  "spec_version": "xenspec/v1",
  "tenant":  { "id": "t_8f3a", "business_name": "Diamond Cutz", "timezone": "America/New_York", "license_key": "SEL-…" },
  "persona": { "name": "Ava", "greeting": "Thanks for calling Diamond Cutz…", "hours": "Mon-Sat 9-7", "voice_id": "…" },
  "channels": [ { "kind": "gmail", "auth": "oauth_ref:or_1a2b" },
                { "kind": "gvoice", "auth": "oauth_ref:or_3c4d" } ],
  "voice":   { "engine": "pipecat", "number": "telnyx_ref:tn_9x", "transport": "telnyx_sip",
               "stt": "whisper_local", "llm": "xen_brain_callback", "tts": "piper_local",
               "ivr": [ { "digit": "1", "action": "book" }, { "digit": "0", "action": "escalate" } ] },
  "deploy":  { "target": "fly", "region": "iad", "size": "shared-1x" },
  "billing": { "close_kit_state": "paid_verified" }
}
```

**Load-bearing rule:** NO raw secrets in the JSON — only `oauth_ref:` / `telnyx_ref:` pointers the control plane resolves at boot. The JSON is idempotent and versioned.

### 3.3 Auto-config steps (configurator = first-boot entrypoint, runs INSIDE the customer's box)

1. **Close-Kit gate** — refuse to boot unless `close_kit_state == paid_verified`.
2. **Verify license** or refuse to boot.
3. **Resolve refs → live tokens** over one mTLS call to the relay; write to the box's own secret store; **tell relay to forget** (momentary custodian — preserves data-ownership, keeps onboarding zero-touch).
4. **Template from in-image files:** `homeserver.yaml` + per-bridge `registration.yaml` + Pipecat bot `.env` + IVR flow.
5. **Boot in dependency order:** Postgres → Synapse → OAuth channels (self-register via token, no QR) → Pipecat voice → inbox (inline-HTML SSE) → Caddy auto-TLS.
6. **Self-verify with auto-rollback:** health probes + **synthetic test-call + round-trip test message**; only on all-green flip to LIVE and post box URL back with a health beacon. HTTP 200 is not the gate — the synthetic call is (canon C17/C32).

Customer sees exactly one screen: **form → "Deploying…" → "Ava is answering your line."**

## 4. Recommended voice layer + deploy command

**Pipecat, self-hosted on Fly.io (Railway fallback), telephony via our existing Telnyx trunk.**

It is the only candidate clearing all load-bearing axes at once: **per-session container isolation** (= true per-customer receptionist, the isolation primitive), **OSS/free-first core** (Pipecat Cloud stays an optional upgrade, never a dependency), **native Telnyx/Twilio SIP** for real IVR + DTMF, and a **config-driven `bot.py`** that maps 1:1 onto `xenspec` env. The M4 stack (`apple-stt-v2.py`, `xen-tts-daemon.swift`, `voice-duplex.py`) stays **Xen's own internal voice** — it is macOS/CoreAudio-bound, one-brain, zero per-tenant isolation, and cannot be the product substrate. Pipecat's LLM node calls **back into the shared Xen brain**, preserving one-voice/one-brain canon.

**Reference bot, stand up now:**
```bash
uv tool install pipecat-ai
pipecat init quickstart      # scaffold + Dockerfile
# add keys to .env  (default: whisper_local STT + piper_local TTS → free-first)
uv run bot.py                # browser voice verify (~20s first-run model pull)
fly launch                   # from generated Dockerfile → per-tenant app
```
Harden into the templated image; the form then drives **`fly deploy` per tenant**.

**Free-first blocker to hold:** default the templated bot to **local Whisper + local Piper + xen_brain LLM callback** — never a per-minute vendor, or the per-min cost reappears one layer down and eats the license margin. Rejected: Vapi/Retell (per-minute recurring, closed — canon C6 violation), M4-as-product (no isolation, no deploy path).

## 6. Three-milestone build plan

**M1 — MVP: one real tenant, end-to-end, zero terminal (target ~6–8 wks).**
Judges were unanimous that "weeks for all six subsystems" is fiction; so M1 scope is deliberately narrow. Ship: static form on hitthe.link → `xenspec/v1` → Close-Kit gate → **OAuth + Telnyx-SMS/RCS + Pipecat-voice ONLY** (no QR bridges) → configurator with dependency-ordered boot + synthetic-call self-verify → one Fly app for ONE real business (Diamond Cutz as design partner). Prove the configurator cold-boots unattended, every time. **Do not build the idempotent-saga-across-6-systems yet** — M1 has 3 side-effecting systems, not 6.

**M2 — First paying B2B client + repeatability (target +4–6 wks).**
Run Close Kit for real (DocuSign+Stripe+Identity+Plaid). Harden the OAuth relay broker (mTLS, per-tenant scoping, revocation, audit). Add the **fleet-update story**: version beacon + pinned GHCR tag + rolling redeploy. Add the **per-channel liveness heartbeat + one-tap self-serve re-consent** (customer-facing, never terminal). Land 1–3 paying tenants. Gate: a tenant provisions with **zero human touching a terminal/QR/keychain**, and a token revocation self-heals or self-serve re-consents.

**M3 — Scale to dozens→100s + the QR question (target quarter+).**
Multi-region Fly, health-ingest dashboard, staggered per-tenant reconnects, drop-rate scorecard as a first-class metric. **Only now** evaluate consumer WhatsApp/Signal — and only as a **proven, paid add-on** after the relay-session lifecycle survives real WhatsApp invalidation unattended for **30 days at multi-tenant scale** (the judges' hard precondition). If it doesn't survive, it never ships as "zero-touch."

## 7. The #1 risk + mitigation

**RISK: The QR-linked consumer-bridge class (WhatsApp/iMessage/Signal) cannot be re-provisioned unattended** — a dead session genuinely needs the account-holder's phone to re-scan, which is exactly the human-in-loop recovery hell the product exists to kill. All three judges named this the single fatal flaw; it is the one auth class we don't own and the one that made recovery unsellable. Shared-host WA sessions additionally carry correlated-ban/ToS risk.

**MITIGATION (build-order, decisive):**
1. **Amputate it from v1, honestly.** No consumer-QR checkbox in the form. v1 = voice + Telnyx SMS/RCS + OAuth channels — **100% machine-refreshable.** The pitch matches the delivery: "zero manual login for every channel we ship."
2. **Prefer machine-refreshable equivalents:** Telnyx SMS/RCS instead of an iMessage device bridge; WhatsApp *Business* Cloud API (service-account grade) instead of consumer QR, where the customer qualifies — with Meta Business verification called out as its own onboarding step, not hidden.
3. **Governing rule, enforced in code:** *every secret must be machine-refreshable or it doesn't ship.* Any channel failing that test is a paid add-on with a re-pair SLA, never a default.
4. **Only after a 30-day live invalidation soak** at multi-tenant scale does any QR-class channel enter the pitch — and then behind a per-provider drop-rate scorecard and a one-tap customer re-pair link (never a terminal, never our SLA silently absorbing the labor).

**Secondary risk to watch (from the winner's own gaps):** unattended configurator cold-boot fragility ("false-green self-verify") — mitigated by the synthetic-call gate in §3.3 step 6 and per-tenant boot isolation, so a wedged boot is one tenant, visible, and auto-rolled-back, never a silent fleet failure.
